Types of bananas

Banana can be whatever color you want, yellow, green, red or blue.

Yellow sweet dessert bananas. The most common are Cavendish Bananas. From unripe green, longer stay in your refrigerator to fully ripe yellow, just pill the skin and eat it. You can add them in smoothies, pancakes, or banana bread. You can find them in supermarkets all over the globe.

Green bananas, green long strange potatoes, plantains. If you want to eat them, you need to cook them first. Tastes like potatoes, cook like poatoes. There is a lot of recepies in the web, most of them are nearly identical to the potato recepies.

Red bananas have red-purple skin, sweet flesh with a pink and orange tinge. When fully ripen, they have a cream to light pink flesh. Tastes like yellow bananas with a slight raspberry flavour.

Blue Java bananas are a hardy, cold-tolerant bananas that have an ice cream-like consistency and flavor reminiscent of vanilla. When they ripe they are yellow.

Where do bananas grow

Bananas loves hot, tropical regions.

In hot, tropical climates across the globe. The most known, sweet bananas, are grow in the Ecuador, Philippines, Costa Rica, Guatemala and Colombia are dominates the global banana export economy. The banana represents an important source of income for these countries.

Typically, Central American bananas are grown on large-scale monoculture plantations covering several hundred hetares each. This form of production uses high levels of chemical inputs to increase crop yield.

Ecuador, Colombia and Peru are the only countries among the major Latin American exporters that still have small banana farmers exporting alongside the large-scale plantations. Across South America, and especially in Peru, some 10,000 small farmers sell to the world market, often thanks to organic and Fairtrade certification.
